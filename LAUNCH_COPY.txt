I've been building an Italian learning project around the idea that beginners shouldn't be stuck with "the cat is on the table" forever.

So I graded classic literature down to A1/A2 using the Kelly Italian Frequency List (6,529 words, CEFR-tagged). The rules:
- One clause per sentence
- Present tense only (past becomes "one day..." + present)
- Concrete > abstract always
- Explicit subjects (never drop "he/she")
